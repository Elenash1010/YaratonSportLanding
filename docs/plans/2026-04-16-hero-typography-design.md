# Hero And Typography Design

## Goal

Make the main hero block fit entirely within the first screen without vertical scrolling, including the three lower metric cards, while shifting the visual tone toward a stricter, more premium look.

## Decisions

- Reduce the global heading scale so `h1` remains assertive but no longer overwhelms the layout.
- Slightly reduce `h2` and `h3` to preserve hierarchy and make the whole site feel more coherent.
- Compress the hero spacing vertically by reducing both the section padding and the inner hero padding/gaps.
- Lower the right-side hero visual height so the two-column composition fits more reliably on laptop screens.
- Compact the action row and lower metric cards so the entire hero composition fits within one viewport.
- Keep the current hero structure and content order; solve the issue through typography and spacing rather than removing elements.

## Expected Outcome

- The hero section fits within the viewport on common desktop laptop heights, including the lower three-card row.
- Headings feel more premium and controlled instead of oversized.
- Other sections inherit a more consistent visual rhythm from the updated heading scale.
